ICIMTech 2026 Submission Checklist
To ensure a smooth submission process and maintain the high standards of the ICIMTech conference, authors are required to adhere to the following guidelines:

1. Originality and Submission Policy
Unpublished Work: Submissions must be original and not under consideration elsewhere.

Withdrawal Policy: Manuscripts cannot be withdrawn after receiving reviewer comments. Violations will result in a two-year submission ban to ICIMTech, and any active manuscripts will be considered withdrawn.

Author Composition: The list and order of authors are final upon submission and cannot be changed thereafter.

2. Authorship Limitations
Paper Limit: Each author may contribute to a maximum of five papers.

Author Count: Each paper can have up to eight authors.

3. Manuscript Formatting
Title: Should be clear, contain no abbreviations, and not exceed 13 words.

Template: Use the official IEEE template for manuscript preparation.

Length: Manuscripts must be between 4 to 6 pages.

Language: All manuscripts must be written in full English and thoroughly proofread for grammatical accuracy.

Scope: Ensure the topic aligns with the conference’s scope.

4. Abstract Requirements
Word Count: Between 150 to 250 words.

Structure: Include Background, Objective, Methods, Results, and Conclusion.

Keywords: Provide 4 to 6 relevant keywords.

5. References and Citations
Quantity: Include at least 15 references.

Quality: At least 80% of references should be from reputable international journals or conference proceedings.

Style: Use IEEE citation style and manage references with automatic citation tools.

6. Originality and Plagiarism
Plagiarism Index: Must not exceed 15%. Papers exceeding this limit will be rejected.

7. AI-Generated Content
Disclosure: Any use of AI-generated content (text, figures, images, code) must be disclosed in the acknowledgements section.

Limit: AI-generated content should not exceed 20% of the manuscript.

8. Figures and Illustrations
Quality: All figures must be high-resolution.

Language: Information within images must be in English. If not, provide English annotations.

We solicit research and experience papers as well as research-in-progress and practitioner reports in any of the technical areas listed under Scope & Topics.

Prospective authors are invited to submit full papers of 4 to 6 pages (including tables, figures, and references) in the standard Conference double-column format (available for download on this page). Papers will be accepted only by electronic submission or Online Submission. Online Submission Website: Paper Submission.

All submitted papers will be evaluated using a double-blind review process. Each complete registration for the conference will cover one paper.

Selection Process
After the submission deadline, each paper’s similarity score is checked. Papers with a high similarity score can not be accepted in our conference. Another important process is the review process by the reviewer. For each paper, a minimum of three reviewers are assigned to review it. The decision to accept a paper is based on the reviewer’s average score and comments. Paper Limit

One person cannot submit more than five papers as an author or co-author.

Guidelines for Artificial Intelligence (AI) – Generated Text
Our policy sets a maximum limit of 20% without acknowledgement. The use of content generated by artificial intelligence (AI) in a paper (including but not limited to text, figures, images, and code) shall be disclosed in the acknowledgements section of any paper submitted to an IEEE publication. The AI system used shall be identified, and specific sections of the paper that use AI-generated content shall be identified and accompanied by a brief explanation regarding the level at which the AI system was used to generate the content. The use of AI systems for editing and grammar enhancement is a common practice and, as such, is generally outside the intent of the above policy. In this case, disclosure, as noted above, is recommended.
